My Experience with Cannabis and Closeness to God: A Journey of Spiritual Discovery

Introduction

In sharing this deeply personal narrative, I find myself at an intersection of vulnerability and revelation. This is not a story of advocacy, but rather an honest account of my spiritual journey—one that has led me through unexpected paths toward a deeper connection with God. As I write these words, I'm acutely aware of the complexity and sensitivity of this topic, yet I feel compelled to share these experiences in hopes that others might find value in my journey of transformation.

Explicit Disclaimer: This narrative is a personal account and does not endorse or promote illegal activities. Cannabis use is illegal in India under the Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985 (NDPS Act), and I do not encourage its use.

The Path of Unexpected Revelation

For a period of time, I walked a path that many might consider contradictory—seeking spiritual enlightenment while engaging in behaviors that are illegal and contrary to spiritual growth. During this period, I experienced moments of profound spiritual connection, spending hours delving into Biblical texts and theological contemplation. While I perceived these experiences as bringing me closer to divine truths, I now recognize that this perception requires careful examination.

What began as curiosity evolved into a pattern of behavior that required reflection and change. A pivotal moment arrived recently when I received divine clarity about my situation.

The Turning Point

I found myself contemplating ways to break free from this habit. This moment of clarity led me to develop a structured plan for change, culminating in a commitment to a life of faith-based living.

Our present sufferings are not worth comparing with the glory that will be revealed in us.
Romans 8:18 ✟

Lessons in Faith and Understanding

The distinction between seeking and finding authentic spiritual connection has become clearer. True spiritual growth often requires clear-minded dedication and genuine surrender.

The journey has taught me about the nature of dependence—both spiritual and physical. What we perceive as spiritual insight must be tested against scripture and sound theological understanding.

Moving Forward

  • Daily scripture study with a clear mind
  • Regular prayer and meditation focused on genuine connection
  • Community engagement with fellow believers
  • Physical and mental health practices that align with spiritual well-being

A Message to Others

To those reading this who might be wrestling with similar experiences or questions, I want to emphasize that this is my personal journey. I share it not as a prescription but as a testimony to God's ability to work through our circumstances—even our mistakes—to draw us closer to Him.

Emphasis on Legal and Healthy Alternatives: I have taken steps toward spiritual growth through lawful means, such as meditation, prayer, and community engagement. These are the practices I now promote and encourage.

Conclusion

This journey has taught me that true spiritual growth often comes through unexpected channels, though not always in the ways we initially perceive. As I move forward, I carry these lessons with gratitude, understanding that every step—even those that led me astray—has contributed to a deeper understanding of faith, redemption, and the endless patience of divine love.

Note to readers: This article is shared with humility and transparency, not as an endorsement of any substance use, but as a testimony to God's ability to guide us toward truth through various life experiences. If you're struggling with substance use, please seek appropriate professional and spiritual guidance.

May God bless you in your spiritual journey. John 3:16-17
